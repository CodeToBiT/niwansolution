System Intregration

System integration is the process of combining and coordinating different subsystems or components of a larger system to ensure they work together as a whole. It involves connecting multiple systems or applications to operate as a single cohesive unit.

System integration can take many forms, such as hardware integration, software integration, or a combination of the two. It can be performed by various entities, including individuals, organizations, or systems integration service providers.

The goal of system integration is to improve the overall efficiency and functionality of the integrated system, by reducing redundancies and increasing productivity. It enables the sharing of data and communication between different subsystems, which helps to reduce errors, streamline operations, and increase the accuracy of data.

Examples of system integration include integrating enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, integrating human resource management (HRM) systems, and integrating customer relationship management (CRM) systems. These integrations help organizations to achieve a better flow of information and more efficient business processes.
